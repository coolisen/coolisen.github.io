How to work faster, how to achieve more, how to simply do more? These are questions that people ask me all the time and that I see being discussed in productivity and performance forums.

You’ll learn a very easy-to-implement 4 step process that you can use to achieve more in the same amount of time (or achieve the same amount of work in less time, you’ll understand what I mean in the video).

This is all based on what of time management’s ultimate law: Parkinson’s Law.

If you’ve heard about it before, you’ll see it used in a completely different and innovative way. If you haven’t come across Parkison’s Law before here’s what it states: work expands so as to fill the time available for its completion. Usually this is seen as not such a positive thing, but you can actually use it to work faster, achieve more and boost your overall productivity.
