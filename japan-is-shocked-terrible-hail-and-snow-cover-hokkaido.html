On the eve, cold air brought the first snow to Wakkanai and other cities in the northernmost prefecture of Hokkaido, Japan. In the city of Sibecha, Hokkaido, the temperature is minus 12 degrees Celsius. Such abnormal cold weather in Japan is recorded not earlier than in the middle of January; therefore, many residents of the province were surprised by such a sharp cold snap and were not ready for this. The weather was fine in most of the country. But in the north of Japan, it snowed along the coast of the Sea of Japan, and in some areas there was a strong destructive hail. This caused great problems with traffic, and also forced the local utilities to throw forces to clear snow from streets and courtyards, as well as roofs of buildings.
