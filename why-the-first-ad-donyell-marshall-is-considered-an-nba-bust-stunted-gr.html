Was Donyell Marshall the first AD? He and Anthony Davis played a similar game, and are usually critiqued for being "too soft". He was before his time growing up in Reading Pennsylvania, and had he come up in an era like today, his game would fit perfectly.
Donyell Marshall was a 6’9 power forward/ small forward that became a parade all American and McDonalds all American by his senior year. He chose the University of Connecticut to play for Jim Calhoun, who promised he’d play immediately and have the chance to be one of the teams top players. He didn’t start off as the most confident or tough players for uconn, usually pushed around with his slender frame and lack of competitive fire. He’d be called on for last shots and routinely ask his coach if he was sure putting him in that position was the right choice. In just a few years he did develop a more mean streak and began to dominate college basketball on his way to a top 5 pick. Was he the first AD? Why is Donyell Marshall considered a BUST!
