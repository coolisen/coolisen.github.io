Why Do Lullabies Put Babies To Sleep?
Lullabies put babies to sleep firstly by giving baby something pleasant to listen to as a distraction from what is going on around them. Babies attention to the music gives baby’s brain a focus - like a meditation.

Secondly the calm sounds and sweet simple melodies of a lullaby relax baby just as they do adults. The lilting 3/4 time rhythm of most lullabies mimics the swaying, rocking movement that babies find so soothing. This helps to regulate emotions if baby is upset or overtired.

Thirdly, listening to a lullaby with your baby or singing a lullaby song to baby is a great way to make and enhance a strong bond between you.

Fourthly, using a lullaby regularly helps to establish a peaceful baby bedtime routine. Listening to the same lullaby can become a signal or cue to your baby that it is time to relax and go to sleep.

Is White Noise Good For Babies?

White noise for babies is not exactly baby lullaby music but it has been proven to help baby sleep. White noise for babies make a baby feel more comfortable as baby is not used to silence. The womb before birth in a noisy place and silence is not what babies are familiar with. White noise really helps sooth and relax an over- tired or colicky infant. It works best with newborns and very young babies. Just be sure not to play is too loud as this can damage baby’s ears.
