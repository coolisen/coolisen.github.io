Speaker: Narasimha Rao, Asst Prof of Energy Systems, Yale School of the Environment

The energy transitions literature focuses on supply-side technologies to the neglect of demand-side options. I will present a vision of a global demand-side transformation, and show evidence from recent research in developing countries to indicate that there is considerable opportunity for mitigating climate change with equitable development. What underlies this vision is a focus on wellbeing rather than economic growth. Recent research derives energy demand from a decent living standards, the material prerequisites to wellbeing. This helps us understand that affluence, rather than basic wellbeing, drives energy growth and related climate impacts. The research also highlights new examples of wellbeing-enhancing mitigation related to cooling, diets and transit in the developing world.
