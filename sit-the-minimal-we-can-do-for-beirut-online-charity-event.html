In a time of economic collapse and political unrest, on 4 August, a massive explosion hit Beirut. A large part of the city became rubble and many lives were lost. Repairs of damage and response to tremendous needs on the ground continue to be overwhelming and insufficiently funded. While priority is given to immediate relief and reconstruction, those most vulnerable such as pregnant and lactating women, children, elderly, people with disabilities, etc have special needs which require dedicated attention. Essential work carried out by local NGOs that requires less investment and provides long term impact for those most excluded and vulnerable, remains underfunded and with winter here, the situation is of growing concern.

Resources gathered will be distributed to local NGOs prioritising people most in need with a high potential of exclusion and discrimination. The projects chosen with support from Impact Lebanon, will aim at providing essential relief, restoring livelihoods and increasing the capacity of small businesses.
