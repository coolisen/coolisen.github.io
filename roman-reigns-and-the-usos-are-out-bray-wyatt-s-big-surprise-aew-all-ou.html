Roman Reigns and The Usos (The Bloodline) are out, Bray Wyatt’s big surprise, AEW All Out Debuts, today’s wrestle news for September 7, 2021


Roman Reigns and The Usos are on top of WWE Smackdown as The Bloodline. Smackdown mostly revolves around Roman Reigns and The Usos. The Usos and Roman Reigns are a part of many Smackdown storylines in WWE, but Smackdown may be without their biggest stars of Roman Reigns and The Usos for their first big UK tour. WWE will be touring the UK with the Smackdown roster but The Bloodline, Roman Reigns and The Usos reportedly won’t be a part of that tour for Smackdown. Roman Reigns is no longer being advertised for the overseas tour and seems to be replaced with Becky Lynch. Roman Reigns and The Usos will instead be joining the WWE Raw roster that week on September 20th. Roman Reigns and The Usos are being advertised to make an appearance on that edition of WWE Raw in North Carolina, it’s currently unknown if Roman Reigns will appear on Raw or if it’ll just be a dark match after Raw goes off air, but The Bloodline will be appearing on that week of Raw.


Roman Reigns and WWE won’t be the only thing we discuss, we’ll talk about the growing popularity of AEW, AEW All Out PPV, and the big AEW debuts of Daniel Bryan (Bryan Danielson), Adam Cole, Ruby Soho (Ruby Riott), and much more. CM Punk vs Darby Allin was CM Punk’s AEW debut match and first wrestling match in over 7 years. Bryan Danielson also spoke at the AEW All Out post show and talked about his decision to leave WWE and sign with AEW. Bryan Danielson also talked about bringing the yes chants to AEW and his relationship with Vince McMahon. With Daniel Bryan and Adam Cole AEW Debut out of the way, We’ll also take a look at possibly AEW’s next big signing, Bray Wyatt. We’ll see the latest on Bray Wyatt on if he can join all the fun over at AEW
